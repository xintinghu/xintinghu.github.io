Project 3: FlexMAN

Flexible Extended Arrival Manager

Project Overview

FlexMAN develops a Human–AI hybrid approach to extended arrival management. It coordinates arrivals earlier and farther upstream, before aircraft enter the destination Flight Information Region (FIR) or congested terminal airspace. The approach combines arrival pre-sequencing, cruise-phase speed advisories, and demand–capacity balancing to distribute delay during the en-route phase and reduce reliance on late tactical intervention near the destination.

The project focuses on fragmented multi-FIR environments such as Southeast Asia, where adjacent airspace may operate under different procedures, service-provider capabilities, and bilateral coordination arrangements without a single regional implementation authority.

Operational Challenge

Conventional arrival management often operates within a short tactical horizon, limiting opportunities to coordinate traffic before it approaches congested terminal airspace.

Short tactical horizon — Late sequencing decisions can lead to arrival bunching and increase the need for tactical intervention close to the destination.

Late delay absorption — Delay may be concentrated through holding, vectoring, or other actions near terminal airspace rather than distributed earlier through cruise-phase adjustments.

Fragmented multi-FIR coordination — Earlier arrival coordination must account for differences in procedures, provider capabilities, information availability, and cross-FIR cooperation arrangements.

My Role

I serve as Algorithm Team Lead, coordinating the work of five research staff and the delivery of the algorithm workstream.

I directly lead research on speed-advisory optimization and demand–capacity balancing. I also coordinate team contributions to data-driven scenario generation, human reaction-time modelling, cross-FIR cooperation modelling, benchmark development and comparison, and algorithm evaluation.

My role further includes coordinating the continuing integration of algorithm modules with the Human–Machine Interface, the simulation environment, and the wider FlexMAN research prototype.

Research Contributions

Speed-Advisory Optimization and Delay Absorption

FlexMAN combines upstream speed adjustment with demand–capacity balancing to support earlier arrival pre-sequencing. The optimization framework identifies cruise-phase speed advisories that can distribute or absorb delay before aircraft reach congested terminal airspace.

Controller Interaction and Operational Feasibility

FlexMAN keeps operational personnel in the decision loop by presenting algorithm-generated speed advisories for controller review. Controllers can accept, reject, or revise a recommendation, after which the system can update its calculations using the accepted decision and current traffic conditions.

From Algorithms to Operational Evaluation

Completed evaluation includes traffic-scenario algorithm assessment and analysis of initial operational-feasibility findings. The studies use representative traffic and airspace scenarios, including cross-FIR conditions relevant to fragmented regional environments.

The evaluations compare proposed coordination methods with current-operation benchmarks and examine the effects of speed-advisory decisions, changing traffic conditions, and controller responses. The findings remain limited to research and simulation settings.

Stakeholder Engagement

FlexMAN is under development through research exchange and stakeholder engagement involving ICAI, MITRE Asia, and GE Aerospace.

Future Research Directions

Future work focuses on three connected directions.

Scalable multi-FIR coordination — Extended arrival-management methods need to remain effective across fragmented airspace with different procedures, provider capabilities, and coordination arrangements. Longer-term work may connect these methods with trajectory-based operations and network-centric air traffic management.

Adaptive demand–capacity balancing — Future methods can incorporate greater uncertainty in traffic demand, arrival capacity, weather conditions, and regional operating constraints.

Explainable Human–AI advisories — Transparent explanations, controller intervention strategies, and models of human response will be important for improving trust and supporting practical decision-making.
