Artificial Intelligence for Air Traffic Management

Project Overview

AI4ATM examines how artificial intelligence can support safer air traffic operations under convective weather conditions. This work explores aircraft trajectory prediction, coordinated rerouting, and safety-constrained multi-agent decision-making.

Operational Challenge

When convective weather makes several planned routes unavailable, independent diversions can create secondary conflicts, inefficient detours, and unstable traffic patterns. Coordinated planning must address three connected requirements:

Uncertainty — Trajectory and ETA errors affect when conflicts emerge and how long a rerouting decision remains valid.

Coordination — Avoiding a storm cell is not sufficient if the revised trajectory creates a new loss of separation with another aircraft.

Safety and generalization — Learning-based methods must produce safe and practical decisions under weather patterns, traffic states, and route structures beyond the original training cases.

My Role

I served as team coordinator and algorithm engineer within the Singapore–Italy collaboration. I coordinated project tasks, deliverables, milestones, reporting, and research alignment between the two national teams, while contributing to operational problem formulation and method development with primary responsibility for scenario design.

I led the policy-shared multi-aircraft rerouting study published at IEEE ICTAI 2025 and co-developed the Advanced Engineering Informatics study on decentralized cooperative trajectory replanning. I also contributed to the technology-disclosure process and the safety-constrained multi-agent research, supporting the early completion of key project performance indicators.

Research Contributions

Policy-Shared Multi-Aircraft Rerouting Under Convective Weather

I led the development and evaluation of a policy-shared deep reinforcement learning method for coordinated rerouting under convective-weather disruptions.

Safety-Constrained Cooperative Trajectory Replanning

I co-developed a decentralized deep reinforcement learning framework in which aircraft make local trajectory decisions while accounting for surrounding traffic and evolving thunderstorm cells.

I also contributed to constrained multi-agent reinforcement learning with MAF-Net, which filters candidate actions against safety constraints before they are applied. Together, these methods extend cooperative replanning toward safer decentralized coordination under dynamic weather and interacting-aircraft risks.

From Methods to Operational Scenarios

The methods were evaluated through simulation scenarios with realistic air-route structures, interacting aircraft, evolving thunderstorm cells, and ETA uncertainty.

Future Research Directions

Three directions extend this work:

  • Generalization — reliable performance under unseen weather patterns, traffic states, and route structures;

  • Safety assurance and explainability — integration of action filtering with verification, interpretable constraints, and recommendations that operational personnel can review;

  • Prediction-informed decision-making — tighter coupling of trajectory prediction, uncertainty estimation, and human-supervised planning as prediction confidence changes.
