Project 1: UAT

Urban Aerial Transport Traffic Management and Systems

Project Overview

UAT examined how Advanced Air Mobility could be integrated into existing aviation systems through risk-aware low-altitude airspace design, third-party risk assessment, flight-plan management, and traffic-management concepts. The programme coordinated four work packages spanning risk, airspace, traffic management, and system integration. I led the Third-Party Risk Assessment work package and coordinated the seven-member team.

Operational Challenge

Low-altitude AAM operations should protect people and property on the ground, maintain separation from other airspace users, and provide airspace that is sufficiently available, accessible, and connected for viable operations. Near aerodromes and in dense urban environments, new routes and procedures should also coexist with conventional traffic, controlled airspace, infrastructure, and existing air traffic management workflows.

The programme addressed four connected challenges:

  • Third-party ground and air risk in low-altitude environments;
  • Airspace availability, accessibility, and connectivity;
  • Aerodrome-adjacent integration with conventional air traffic and operational restrictions;
  • Strategic conflict management and integration with existing ATM procedures.

My Role

I translated agency and stakeholder requirements into research questions, methods, and implementation priorities; coordinated work spanning data analytics, risk modelling, algorithm design, software development, and simulation; and connected research models with stakeholder-facing prototypes. I also supervised postgraduate and undergraduate research activities associated with the programme.

Research Contributions

Risk-Aware Airspace and Route Planning

I developed spatial-temporal risk representations and applied them to risk-aware route planning and safety-constrained airspace design. The methods supported feasible-route analysis under risk limits and were implemented in the AAM Third-Party Risk Platform.

Airspace Availability, Accessibility, and AAM Integration

I developed methods to assess airspace availability and accessibility, including near aerodromes, and analysed constraints for door-to-door air transportation and coexistence with conventional aviation. The results informed airspace reconfiguration studies and the DDAT analysis framework.

Flight-Plan and Traffic Management

I contributed methods for flight-plan management, strategic conflict management, and AAM–ATM integration, linking traffic interactions in constrained airspace to implementable planning logic and prototype functions.

Research Translation and Prototypes

Research methods were translated into three integrated research outputs whose scope and evaluation were shaped by agency and stakeholder requirements.

AAM Third-Party Risk Platform — A research platform connecting third-party ground and air risk models with airspace and route analysis.

AAM flight-plan management prototype — A prototype supporting flight-plan coordination and strategic conflict management for low-altitude AAM operations.

Door-to-door air transportation analysis framework — A framework for analysing airspace connectivity and operational requirements for door-to-door air transportation.

Future Research Directions

UAT provides a foundation for future research on scalable risk-aware AAM integration, dynamic airspace accessibility, door-to-door air transportation, and aerodrome integration. Key directions include:

  • Dynamic airspace management incorperating ground and air risks;
  • Human–AI decision support for low-altitude operational planning and control.

Long-term plan. To make AAM safe to operate, manageable at scale, and capable of supporting a sustainable low-altitude mobility ecosystem.
