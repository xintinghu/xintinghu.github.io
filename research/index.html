Research

My research develops risk-aware and AI-enabled methods for integrating Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) into existing aviation systems and improving the safety and resilience of air traffic operations.

Across both application domains, human–AI collaborative decision support connects operational problem formulation, risk and traffic modelling, optimization and AI methods, decision-support prototypes, and stakeholder-informed evaluation.

THEME 1

Low-Altitude Air Mobility and Airspace Integration

This theme develops risk-aware airspace designs and operational frameworks for integrating AAM and other low-altitude operations into existing aviation systems.

Problem Statement

Low-altitude airspace is accommodating novel flight operations while maintaining safety for people and property on the ground, conventional traffic, and other airspace users.

The central challenge is to balance risk, airspace availability, accessibility, and connectivity across locations and time, especially near aerodromes and controlled airspace.

Research Directions and Methods

Risk-aware airspace design and reconfiguration

Model third-party ground and air risk to support spatial and temporal airspace decisions.

Airspace availability and accessibility

Assess where and when low-altitude operations can be accommodated under operational and safety constraints.

Door-to-door and flight-plan integration

Connect route design, flight-plan management, traffic-flow management, and system-level accessibility for door-to-door operations.

Aerodrome and controlled-airspace integration

Develop operational frameworks for interactions among AAM, conventional traffic, controlled airspace, and operational restrictions.

THEME 2

Safe and Resilient Air Traffic Management

This work develops AI-enabled coordination and recovery methods for safer and resilient air traffic operations under uncertainty, weather disruptions, and demand–capacity imbalances.

Problem

Convective weather, capacity constraints, and multi-aircraft interactions create rapidly changing traffic conditions.

Effective management requires timely coordination, rerouting, replanning, and recovery while maintaining safety and operational feasibility.

Research Directions and Methods

Disruption-aware trajectory management

Represent dynamic weather and uncertainty in multi-aircraft planning.

Multi-aircraft coordination and rerouting

Apply optimization and multi-agent reinforcement learning for cooperative trajectory decisions.

Demand–capacity balancing and regional flow management

Coordinate traffic across terminal areas and flight information region boundaries.

Recovery under operational constraints

Develop rapid replanning and advisory methods for disrupted traffic conditions.

THEME 3 · CROSS-CUTTING DIRECTION

Human–AI Collaborative Decision Support

Human–AI collaborative decision support connects the two application domains by translating operational models and AI methods into systems that remain understandable, reviewable, and usable by human operators.

Why This Matters

In safety-critical aviation, algorithmic performance alone is not sufficient. Recommendations must support human oversight, explain their operational consequences, and be evaluated with the stakeholders who will use them.

Research Directions and Methods

Explainable operational recommendations

Communicate why an advisory is proposed, which constraints it addresses, and what operational trade-offs it introduces.

Human-in-the-loop workflows

Support human review, modification, acceptance, or rejection of AI-generated recommendations.

AI-assisted decision support with human oversight

Combine algorithmic outputs with operational context and human expertise.

Stakeholder-informed prototype evaluation

Assess usability, workload, operational feasibility, and acceptance through simulations and experiments.

Programme Connections

UAT contributes risk-aware airspace and flight-plan models for low-altitude operations.

AI4ATM contributes multi-aircraft coordination and rerouting methods under weather disruptions.

FlexMAN provides the most direct operational setting for studying human–AI collaboration through arrival-management advisories and stakeholder-informed evaluation.

Future Research Directions

Building on these foundations, my future research will move from individual methods and prototypes toward integrated, scalable, and operationally deployable systems in three directions.

01

Dynamic and Assured AAM Integration

Advance from scenario-based airspace assessment toward dynamic and scalable airspace management with runtime safety assurance for high-density AAM operations, integrating third-party risk, airspace accessibility, door-to-door connectivity, and aerodrome interfaces.

02

Network-Scale Resilient Air Traffic Management

Extend aircraft- and flow-level methods toward proactive, network-scale coordination across FIRs, combining uncertainty-aware prediction, distributed decision-making, and rapid recovery under weather and capacity disruptions.

03

Adaptive Human–AI Teaming

Move from explainable advisory prototypes toward adaptive human–AI teaming, where explanation, decision authority, and levels of automation respond to operational risk, traffic complexity, and human workload.
