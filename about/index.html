About

I am a Research Fellow at the Air Traffic Management Research Institute, Nanyang Technological University, with a research focus on AI and intelligent air transportation. My work studies low-altitude air mobility, air traffic management, and connects analytical methods with decision-support tools and research prototypes designed for real aviation challenges.

Research Trajectory

Low-Altitude Flight Operations. I began by studying risk assessment and path planning for unmanned aircraft in urban environments. This established my interest in exploring the safety issues of low-altitude mobility as a system-level challenge.

System Integration. During my doctoral research, I expanded from individual aircraft operations to shared airspace and system-level integration, including the interaction between emerging air mobility and existing air traffic management systems. These studies deepened my understanding of how airspace structures, safety constraints, and traffic-management mechanisms can be designed together.

Resilient ATM. My recent work addresses multi-aircraft coordination, disrupted operations, traffic-flow management, and AI-enabled decision support. Across these areas, I study how operational constraints, artificial intelligence, and human judgement can support implementable decisions in increasingly complex aviation systems.

Looking Ahead

I aim to build an independent research program connecting emerging air mobility, resilient management of complex and disrupted traffic, and trustworthy human–AI decision support for airspace and aviation stakeholders.

My future work will develop explainable and adaptable AI methods evaluated through realistic scenarios, stakeholder-informed studies, research prototypes, and human-in-the-loop experiments.
